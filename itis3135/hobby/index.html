Home

The RC car hobby is all about remote controlled RC cars. There are so many different kinds and sizes of RC cars. In this hobby, not only do you drive the cars, you bash em, break em, fix em, and upgrade em. Of course there are people who are a little more gentle with their cars and prefer to race on tracks and the like but there arent any tracks nearby. So me and my RC buddies tend to find some open land, slap some jumps together and send our cars to the moon!

Of course, this hobby is only so much fun by yourself. If you're considering getting into the hobby, make sure to bully your friends into getting one too so you have some driving buddies!

Seasons

The best time to pick up your remote and start driving has got to be the Summer break. It's the perfect weather, usually nice and dry, and there's no school. This is when me and my friends drive our cars the most and if you get into the hobby, you should take advantage of the Summer while it lasts too.

    Positives of Summer

  • Nice warm weather
  • Not too much rain and mud
  • No school
  • Your driving buddies are more likely to be free

    • Negatives of Summer

  • Most of the good spots to drive are crowded with other people at this time
  • Can sometimes be too hot

Best location

Since there aren't any RC tracks in the area, you have to get a little creative. But honestly, this just means that everywhere is a track. By far, my favourite spots to drive are construction sites while nobody is around. It's all loose dirt and with all the piles of dirt and rocks around, you have plenty of jumps at your disposal. The only down side to this is that you need a larger car to handle that kind of terrain and it's pretty easy to break your car if you're not careful.

If you're having trouble finding constructions sites to dived, the next best location I can reccomend are public parks. One of the parks near my has a giant soccerfield and multiple baseball fields and there is almost always at least one field available at any given time. If you're dead set on constructions sites, try going to levelset.com and searching near your location to find potential driving sites.

How to enter the hobby

If you're interested in this hobby, it's really easy to get into. The only drawback is that it's quite expensive. Any of the good cars will run you hundreds of dollars. My favourite place to browse is Hobby Town USA which has plenty of great cars in store. Once you get your first car, all you have to do is charge it up and start ripping! If it breaks, since these are expenive hobby grade cars, all parts are availble to cheap at your local hobby store or online. All you need are come allen keys.

If you're looking for a great relatively affordable RC cars, you can always ask around at your local hobby shop for reccomendations. If you ask me personally, I highly reccomend going 4 wheel drive. It's a little harder to work on but it's a much better driving experience. Two cars I can reccomend to start out with are the 4x4 brushed traxxas slash or the 4wd brushed traxxas rustler. Entry price there is around $300 but those cars handle good, take an absolute beating, and have plenty or parts available everywhere.

Why is this my hobby

I first got into the hobby thanks to my dad who grew up building and driving RC cars. I've seen and messsed with plenty of RC cars in my days. I even own a gas powered one. But I really love this hobby because it's a great way for me to get outdoors and have some fun. I also get to do some really crazy things with my cars, and since I'm not sitting in the driver seat, I'm perfectly safe. It's also a lot more affordable than real cars.

I mainly reccomend this hobby to those who have someone to drive with, be that family or friend. There is also a huge community online and plenty of facebook groups for you to join and learn more about the hobby. It's really wholesome and everyone involved is really kind and willing tell you more about the hobby and their cars. That and the fact that you can really personalize your car to be your own and unique is why I got into thos hobby and have such a great time. This hobby gets you outdoors and spending time with others and gives you something to wrench on when you get home from an RC trip.
